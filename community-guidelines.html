Community Guidelines

Welcome to PromptVerse! To ensure a positive, creative, and collaborative environment for everyone, please read and follow these guidelines. Our community thrives on respect, quality, and shared learning.

Our Philosophy

PromptVerse is built on the idea that sharing and refining prompts collectively leads to better creativity and innovation for everyone. We believe in:

  • Openness: Freely sharing prompts and ideas.
  • Collaboration: Working together to improve content.
  • Respect: Valuing diverse perspectives and contributions.
  • Quality: Striving for clear, useful, and inspiring prompts.

Acceptable Content

When creating or editing prompts and categories, please adhere to the following:

Be Respectful

  • Do not post content that is defamatory, abusive, harassing, threatening, or an invasion of a right of privacy of another person.
  • Avoid hate speech, discriminatory remarks, or content that promotes violence against individuals or groups based on race, ethnic origin, religion, disability, gender, age, veteran status, or sexual orientation/gender identity.

Be Original or Properly Attributed

  • Share prompts that you've created or that are freely available. If you're sharing a prompt inspired by someone else's work, give credit where it's due if possible.
  • Do not upload content that infringes on copyrights, trademarks, or intellectual property rights of others.

Be Clear and Useful

  • Prompts should be understandable and provide enough context for others to use them effectively.
  • Categories should be well-defined and help users find relevant prompts.

No Harmful Content

  • Do not post content that is obscene, pornographic, or sexually explicit.
  • Avoid content that promotes illegal activities or substances.
  • No malware, viruses, or other malicious code.

No Spam or Excessive Self-Promotion

  • Content should be focused on prompts and their usage.
  • Avoid excessive advertising, affiliate links, or irrelevant self-promotion. Linking to your own relevant projects is acceptable if done sparingly and in context.

Creating & Editing Prompts

Quality over Quantity

Aim to create prompts that are thoughtful and well-constructed. A few high-quality prompts are more valuable than many low-quality ones.

Provide Context

When creating a prompt, include a clear title and, if helpful, a brief description of its purpose, how to use it, or any specific variables users might need to fill in.

Use Clear Language

Write prompts in clear, concise language. Avoid jargon where possible, or explain it if necessary. Ensure your prompt is grammatically correct and easy to understand.

Creating & Editing Categories

Uniqueness and Relevance

Before creating a new category, check if a similar one already exists. New categories should fill a genuine need and be distinct from existing ones.

Clear Naming and Description

Category names should be concise and accurately reflect the content within. Provide a clear description for the category to help users understand its scope.

Appropriate Icon and Color

Choose an icon and color that visually represent the category. This helps with navigation and makes the platform more user-friendly.

Review Process

Initial Submission

When you submit a new prompt, it becomes immediately available to the community. New categories, however, undergo a review process.

Community Voting (for New Categories)

Newly created categories are placed in a "Pending Approval" section. Community members can vote on these categories. A category typically needs a certain number of upvotes (e.g., 5 net upvotes) to be considered for approval.

Moderator Review

Our volunteer moderators review pending categories that have met the community voting threshold. They check for adherence to guidelines, uniqueness, and overall quality. Moderators may also review flagged prompts or edits.

Moderator Discretion: Moderators reserve the right to edit, merge, or remove categories and prompts that violate guidelines or are detrimental to the platform's quality, even if they have received community upvotes.

Editing Etiquette

Constructive Edits

When editing a prompt or category, aim to improve its clarity, usefulness, or accuracy. Edits should add value to the content.

Explain Your Changes

When making significant edits, provide a brief reason for your changes. This helps the original creator and other community members understand the rationale behind the modifications.

Respect Original Intent

While improvements are encouraged, try to respect the original intent of the prompt or category creator. Avoid drastically changing the core idea unless it's clearly flawed or problematic.

Community Moderation System

Reporting Content

If you encounter a prompt or category that violates these guidelines, please use the "Report" feature. Provide a clear reason for your report so moderators can take appropriate action.

Voting System

Your votes on pending categories help shape the platform. Upvote categories you find useful and well-defined. Downvote categories that are unclear, redundant, or violate guidelines. Constructive feedback in comments is also encouraged.

Moderator Roles

Moderators are trusted community members who volunteer their time to help maintain the quality and integrity of PromptVerse. They review reported content, approve new categories, and help resolve disputes.

Becoming a Moderator: Active and helpful community members who consistently demonstrate good judgment and adherence to guidelines may be invited to become moderators.

Consequences of Violations

Violations of these guidelines may result in:

  • Removal or editing of the offending content.
  • A warning from moderators.
  • Temporary or permanent suspension of account privileges, including the ability to submit or edit content.
  • In severe cases, a permanent ban from PromptVerse.

Decisions are made at the discretion of the moderation team, based on the severity and frequency of violations.
